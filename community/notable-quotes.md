# Notable Quotes

Lines that stuck from the Coding Agents Lunch & Learn series. Real talk from practitioners building with AI agents every day.

---

## On the Nature of Working with AI Agents

> "We're all our own little R&D departments these days."
> — **Demetrios Brinkmann**

> "As AI agents get smarter, think of how you work with them less like programming, more like working with people."
> — **Rob Ennals**

> "You shouldn't micromanage your people, you shouldn't micromanage your agents."
> — **Rob Ennals**

> "So much of what it means to manage agents well right now is what it means to manage people well, because the smarter they get, the more they behave like people."
> — **Rob Ennals**

> "Your job is not to build the product. Your job is to build the team that builds the product."
> — **Rob Ennals**

## On Practical Limits

> "Once you're managing more than two or three, it's like, I can't even remember what I'm doing here anymore."
> — **Justin Kellam**

> "I cannot have brain cycles enough for more than three. So I'm always stuck with just max 3 agents."
> — **Rahul Parundekar**

> "Beyond 2-3 agents for front end, beyond 2-3 agents for back end together — from my experience, it doesn't work that well."
> — **Tanmay Tiwari**

## On Burnout and Human Attention

> "You will see a lot of burnout happening in 5-6 months. The reasoning is it's like a machine gun attack coming to you."
> — **Tanmay Tiwari**

> "If you're having to manually check what's breaking, what's not breaking? You've got a problem in your process."
> — **Rob Ennals**

> "There's a lot of value in getting humans out of the loop. Any time you find yourself needing to check something, approve something, walk through — how could I have not needed to do this?"
> — **Rob Ennals**

> "In the era of AI-driven code, you can write more code than humans can review. If you expect humans to review it, they're going to say they reviewed it and just click approve."
> — **Rob Ennals**

> — **Demetrios Brinkmann**

> "In the olden days of artisanal handcrafted code, making a bad architectural decision wasted tons of work. Now refactoring is cheap."
> — **Rob Ennals**

> "A picture is worth 1000 words — it's worth at least 1000 tokens for sure."
> — **Leo Walker**
